#### Skill 5: Behavioral / Culture Fit

```
STAR Method for every answer:
- Situation: What was the context?
- Task: What was your responsibility?
- Action: What did YOU do? (not "we")
- Result: What happened? (include NUMBERS)

Prepare 6 stories from your 1.5 years:

Story 1: "Biggest technical challenge"
  → What was hard, how you solved it, what you learned

Story 2: "Time you disagreed with someone"
  → Showed you can push back respectfully + reach resolution

Story 3: "Time you failed"
  → Shows self-awareness, learning from failure

Story 4: "Time you went above and beyond"
  → Initiative, ownership beyond your role

Story 5: "Time you had to make a decision with incomplete information"
  → Shows judgment, ability to act under ambiguity

Story 6: "Time you improved a process or system"
  → Impact, proactivity, engineering mindset

Company-specific:
- Amazon: 14 Leadership Principles (must know ALL, map stories to principles)
  → Customer Obsession, Ownership, Bias for Action, Earn Trust, Dive Deep...
- Google: "Googliness" — teamwork, humility, ethical thinking
- Meta: Move Fast, Be Bold, Focus on Impact
- Stripe: Users First, rigor, clarity of thought
- Atlassian: "Don't #@!% the customer", Open company no BS, Play as a team

Action: Write each story in detail (300-500 words each) and practice telling them aloud.
```

## Part 4: Company-Specific Preparation

### Stripe (SDE-1: 56 LPA)

```
Rounds: Recruiter → Phone Screen → Onsite (5 rounds)

UNIQUE rounds you won't find elsewhere:
1. Bug Bash: Fix bugs in a real codebase using debugger

   → Tools: IntelliJ debugger, breakpoints, step-through

2. Integration Round: Read API docs, integrate with external service
   → Practice: build a project that calls Stripe/Twilio/SendGrid APIs
   → Skills: HTTP client, JSON parsing, error handling, file I/O

3. Coding: Pair-programming style, clean code matters MORE than speed
   → They care about code quality, not just correctness

Prep focus:
```

## Part 5: Application Strategy

### When to Apply (Don't Wait!)

```
                    Apply                        Interview
Week 6  ──────────→ Tier 3 (Indian) ──────────→ Week 8-10
Week 8  ──────────→ Tier 4 (Remote)  ──────────→ Week 10-12
Week 10 ──────────→ Tier 2 (Uber, Atlassian) ──→ Week 12-14
Week 12 ──────────→ Tier 1 (Stripe, Google) ───→ Week 14-16+

Why early companies first:
1. Real interviews > mock interviews for learning
2. You WILL bomb early interviews — better at warm-up companies
3. Confidence builds with each interview
4. Scheduling takes 2-4 weeks
5. You might get offers to use as leverage!
```

### How to Get Interviews

```
Channel               Success Rate    Action
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Employee Referral     30-50%          Network on LinkedIn, ask college alumni
Direct Application    5-10%           Apply on company career page
LinkedIn Easy Apply   2-5%            Mass apply but low conversion
Recruiters reaching   Varies          Update LinkedIn, set "Open to Work"
Hiring platforms      10-20%          Instahyre, Naukri, Cutshort

REFERRAL STRATEGY:
1. Find employees on LinkedIn (search "Company + SDE")
2. Send connection request with SHORT message:
   "Hi [Name], I'm preparing for SDE roles at [Company].
   I've been building [brief project]. Would you be open
   to a quick chat about the interview process?"
3. After connecting, ask if they can refer you
4. Send them your resume + job link
5. Most companies pay referral bonuses — people WANT to refer

Apply to 8-10 companies simultaneously:
- You want multiple offers for negotiation leverage
- Some will ghost you, some will reject — it's a numbers game
- Target: 50+ applications total over 4-6 weeks
```

## Part 8: Negotiation (When You Get Offers)

```
GOLDEN RULES:
1. NEVER give your current salary (say "I'd prefer to discuss based on the role")
2. NEVER accept the first offer
3. Always ask: "Is there flexibility on the compensation?"
4. If you have multiple offers — mention it (creates urgency)
5. Negotiate base, stock, signing bonus, and joining date separately

Script when asked for expectations:
"I'm targeting roles in the [X-Y LPA] range based on the market rate
for this role at companies of similar size. I'm flexible and open to
discussing what works for both of us."

Script when you get an offer:
"Thank you for the offer! I'm very excited about [Company]. I'd like
to take a couple of days to review the details. Is there flexibility
on [base/stock/signing bonus]?"

Leverage:
- Multiple offers = best leverage
- "I have an offer from [Company X] at [amount], and while I prefer
  [your company], I want to make sure the numbers work."
- Companies EXPECT negotiation — you're not being rude
```
